    Spotlight 1: Sergey Brin

    Sergey Brin was born in Moscow, Russia on August 21, 1973 and escaped Jewish persecution 1979. At Stanford University he met Larry Page and together they created Google. Since its launch in 1998, Google is now the most popular search engine in the world.

    Spotlight 2: Anousheh Ansari

    Anousheh Ansari was born on September 12, 1966 in Meshed, Iran and is a Irian-born American buisnesswoman. As a teenager she emigrated from Iran to the United States and got her bachelor's degree in electronics and computer engineering from George Mason University. After getting her masters from George Washington University, she became the first Muslim woman to go into space and the became the first woman space tourist.

    Spotlight 3: Madeline Albright

    Madeline Albright was born on May 15,1937 in Prague,Czechoslovakia. When the Nazis invaded Czechoslovakia, her famiy fled to England and then in 1948, they settled in the United States. Albright worked her way up to serve as a US ambassador to the United Nation and was the first woman to hold the cabinet post of US secretary of state.

    Spotlight 4: Freddy Adu

    Freddy Adu is a soccer player from Ghana. When he was 8 years old, his mother won a Green Card Lottery, which allowed them to immigrate to the US. When Adu was 14, he became the youngest athlete ever to sign a contract in the US. Soon, he becomes the youngest player and scorer in Major League Soccer history.

    Spotlight 5: Elie Wiesel

    Elie Wiesel was born on September 30, 1928 in Sighet, Romania. He was concetration camp survivor from World War II and came to the US. He became a Romian-born Jewish writer and was awarded the Nobel Prize for Peace in 1986.

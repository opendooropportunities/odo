    When Jin Park was seven years old, he and his parents arrived in New York City and grew up in Queens, NY. Now, at age 22, he is a senior at Harvard working towards a degree in molecular and cellular biology.He made history by becoming the first recipient of Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals to earn a Rhodes Scholarship.

    Reshma Saujani is the Founder and CEO of the international nonprofit organization called Girls Who Code which is aimed to close the gender gap in technology. She was also the first Indian American woman to run for US Congress. Because of her work on behalf of young women, she has earned a broad recognition and was the winner of the Harold W. McGraw,Jr. Prize in Education.

    Ana Roca Castro is a social entrepreneur and the CEO of PreK12 Plaza which is the first multicultural education platform. This platform is dedicated to close the academic achievement gap among children of immigrants and for students who are at risk. She also created Lationos in Social Media which is the largest organization of Latino professional who are engaged in social media.

    Alyssia Jovellanos studies Computer Science at McMaster University and is an undergraduate teaching assistant there. She came from a high school that didn't offer computer science courses, but she was the only girl out of her whole entire graduating class to pursue in this career. She was also elected as the youngest and first female president of her university's Computer Science Society. At the age of 17, she was the founder and owner of her multimedia technology company called Beta Pear.

    Albert Einstein was born in Wurttemberg, Germany on March 14, 1879. He was a German-born physicist who made the famous theories of relativity. He also won the Nobel Prize for Physics in 1921 and is now one of the most influential physicists in the 20th century.
